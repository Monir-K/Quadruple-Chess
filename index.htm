Rules Guide

Goal

Defend your own king and outlast the other three sides. A player is eliminated when their king is lost or when they are resolved as checkmated by the game.

Board And Turn Order

  • The game is played on a 4-player cross board.
  • Turn order is Green, Blue, Red, then Yellow.
  • Each player has an individual clock.

Special Rules In This Version

  • Castling is supported when the king and rook have not moved, the path is clear, and the king does not pass through check.
  • Entering the central zone can trigger optional promotion for a normal pawn.
  • When a pawn crosses the center and enters enemy territory, it becomes a zombie pawn.
  • Reaching the final promotion line allows normal promotion choices.
  • If only kings remain, the game is a draw.
  • If two players remain and one side only has a king, the stronger side has 7 moves to force mate or the game is drawn.

How Zombie Pawns Work

  • Zombie pawns move and attack with special rules used by this game variant.
  • They keep the pawn body but show a zombie face so they are easy to spot.

Game Setup

  • You can choose each player as Human or AI.
  • You can rename all four players.
  • You can enable or disable sound before starting.

About Me

I often observed that when two friends played chess, their other companions watched eagerly, hoping for a chance to jump in with their own strategies. Some hesitated to offer advice, while others unintentionally revealed plans by suggesting moves or warning about risks. This game solves that dynamic, eliminating the loneliness of bystanders in a group setting. Quadruple Chess allows everyone to participate in a truly social format. I've infused it with extra adrenaline and excitement—though not recommended for those over 74-80, depending on heart condition.
